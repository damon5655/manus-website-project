## Approach A: Brutalist Cinema Noir

**Design Movement:** Neo-Brutalism meets Film Noir
**Core Principles:**
- Raw, unfiltered visual tension — no softening of edges
- Typographic dominance: oversized type as primary visual element
- Grain and noise as texture, not decoration
- Asymmetric weight distribution

**Color Philosophy:** Pure black (#000000) background with warm cream (#E1E0CC) text. The contrast is stark and intentional — like a silver gelatin print. No gradients except as cinematic vignettes.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed sections with type that bleeds off-screen. The hero heading "Prisma" occupies 20vw+ and anchors the bottom of the viewport, creating a monumental feeling.

**Signature Elements:**
- Superscript asterisk (*) as brand punctuation
- Noise overlay on video for analog film texture
- Black pill navbar hanging from the top edge

**Interaction Philosophy:** Animations are deliberate and slow — pull-up text reveals feel like a curtain rising. Scroll-linked character opacity creates a typewriter-like reading experience.

**Animation:** Words slide up from y:20 with staggered 0.08s delays. Card entrances scale from 0.95 with ease [0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]. Scroll-linked character opacity for body text.

**Typography System:** Almarai (300/400/700/800) as global sans — geometric and clean. Instrument Serif italic for accent phrases — creates a cultured, editorial contrast.

## Approach B: Analog Warmth / Darkroom Aesthetic

**Design Movement:** Darkroom Photography + Editorial Print
**Core Principles:**
- Warmth within darkness — not cold tech, but human craft
- Text as image: typography treated with the same care as photography
- Layered depth: video + noise + gradient create dimensional space
- Restraint in color, richness in texture

**Color Philosophy:** The cream palette (#DEDBC8, #E1E0CC) evokes aged photographic paper. Against pure black, it reads as luminous — like a print emerging in developer solution.

**Layout Paradigm:** Bottom-anchored hero content creates a cinematic "title card" effect. The 12-column grid splits narrative (left) from action (right) — editorial magazine logic.

**Signature Elements:**
- Full-viewport video with analog noise overlay
- Progressive character reveal on scroll (like developing film)
- Feature cards as "contact sheets" — uniform, systematic, grid-based

**Interaction Philosophy:** The page reveals itself as you scroll — content emerges rather than appearing. Every interaction has weight and consequence.

**Animation:** Pull-up reveals with custom ease [0.16, 1, 0.3, 1] — organic deceleration. Scroll-linked opacity creates a reading rhythm that matches natural eye movement.

**Typography System:** Almarai for structural text, Instrument Serif italic for the "voice" — the personal, human moments in the copy.

## Approach C: Cinematic Minimalism

**Design Movement:** Minimalist Cinema / Arthouse Film Title Sequence
**Core Principles:**
- Every element earns its place — ruthless editing
- Motion as meaning: animations communicate, not decorate
- Scale as hierarchy: the giant "Prisma" heading IS the design
- Silence and space as active ingredients

**Color Philosophy:** Black is not empty — it is presence. The cream text (#E1E0CC) is the only light source. This creates a spotlight effect where text feels projected onto darkness.

**Layout Paradigm:** Sections are full-viewport experiences, not scrollable content blocks. Each section is a "scene" with its own visual logic and pacing.

**Signature Elements:**
- Noise texture as film grain — connecting digital to analog
- The asterisk superscript as a footnote to the brand name
- Feature cards as a filmstrip — sequential, numbered, systematic

**Interaction Philosophy:** The interface rewards patience. Animations are not instant gratification — they unfold at their own pace, asking the viewer to slow down.

**Animation:** Staggered word reveals create a reading tempo. Card entrances use spring physics. Scroll-linked text opacity creates a meditative reading experience.

**Typography System:** Almarai's geometric clarity for body and UI. Instrument Serif's italic warmth for the human voice. Size contrast is extreme — from 20vw display to 10px labels.
